Online ESR Estimation of Boost Converter Output Capacitor

Condition Monitoring & Fault Prediction in Power Electronics

⚠️

1. Purpose & Problem Statement

As capacitors age, their Equivalent Series Resistance (ESR) inevitably increases. In a boost converter, an elevated ESR degrades the system by causing higher output voltage ripples, severe efficiency drops, and accelerated thermal stress. Left unchecked, this ultimately leads to catastrophic converter failure.

The Existing Problem: Standard ESR measurement techniques require disconnecting the capacitor from the circuit or using bulky external test equipment (like LCR meters), forcing system downtime. There is a critical need for an online, non-invasive health monitoring alternative.

📋

2. Project Summary

This project successfully develops an online health monitoring system implemented in MATLAB/Simulink. By leveraging the naturally occurring voltage ripple generated during the boost converter's normal switching cycles, the system extracts real-time health data. The core methodology isolates the specific voltage step caused by the ESR, enabling seamless, live condition tracking and proactive fault prediction without disrupting operation.

⚙️

3. Implementation Process

The complete non-invasive signal processing pipeline built inside Simulink follows these key sequential stages:

1

Ripple Measurement & Filtering

The output voltage ripple is safely isolated, amplified, and passed through a low-pass filter to eliminate high-frequency noise.

2

Equivalent Time Sampling (ETS)

A Zero-Order Hold ADC structure employs ETS to reconstruct the high-speed switching ripple waveform accurately at a lower physical sampling cost.

3

Waveform Extraction & Detection

Unit Delay and Sum blocks capture individual waveform shifts, while a Threshold Detection block identifies the critical ESR-driven voltage step.

4

MATLAB Math Computation

A mathematical function block dynamically computes the final capacitor ESR based on the isolated step voltage and converter switching states.
